Projects

Connected Greenhouse (IoT)

Microcontroller Sensors C/C++ Python MQTT/HTTP Power‑aware design

I designed and built a small IoT‑enabled greenhouse that monitors temperature, humidity, soil moisture and light, and automates watering/ventilation. The system logs data for analysis and alerts users to abnormal conditions.

Microelectronics

  • Sizing the power stage (battery/USB), regulators, fuses, and protection.
  • Wiring digital/analog sensors (I²C/SPI/UART, ADCs) and actuators (pumps, fans via PWM & MOSFETs).
  • Noise‑aware routing and basic EMC hygiene; connectors and strain relief.

Programming

  • Development on Arduino Uno using C/C++ (sensors control, data acquisition, automation logic).
  • Design and wiring of electronic circuits (sensors, actuators, relays, fans, valves).
  • Integration of environmental sensors (temperature, humidity, soil moisture, light) and calibration of readings.
  • Implementation of control logic for automated greenhouse management (fans, watering, ventilation).

Sustainability & Design

  • Material choice with durability and reparability in mind.
  • Low‑power strategies (sleep modes, duty cycling, adaptive sampling).
  • Modular enclosure for easy maintenance and upgrades.

Outcome: stable 24/7 operation, clear dashboards, and healthier plants — while keeping cost, energy and e‑waste in check.
