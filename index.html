Projects

Connected Greenhouse (IoT)

Microcontroller Sensors C/C++ Python MQTT/HTTP Power‑aware design

I designed and built a small IoT‑enabled greenhouse that monitors temperature, humidity, soil moisture and light, and automates watering/ventilation. The system logs data for analysis and alerts users to abnormal conditions.

Microelectronics

  • Designed and wired the full electronic system using an Arduino Uno.
  • Integrated environmental sensors (temperature, humidity, soil moisture) through analog and digital interfaces (I²C, analog inputs).
  • Controlled actuators such as the fan, pump, and solenoid valve via relays and MOSFETs.
  • Managed power distribution with regulated 5 V / 12 V lines, fuses, and secure cable routing.
  • Troubleshot wiring and power issues to improve system reliability and safety.

Programming

  • Developed automation code in C/C++ (Arduino IDE) for ventilation and irrigation control.
  • Implemented threshold-based logic: fan activation from temperature data and drip irrigation triggered by soil moisture readings.
  • Calibrated sensors and refined code stability by resolving library and timing conflicts.
  • Structured modular functions to simplify debugging and scalability.
  • Logged and analyzed environmental data for testing and performance validation.

Sustainability & Design

  • Applied an eco-design approach using recyclable and lightweight materials (wood, plexiglass, 3D-printed PLA parts).
  • Designed a compact 50×50×50 cm modular structure for easy assembly, maintenance, and accessibility.
  • Reduced energy and water consumption through optimized fan control and drip irrigation systems.
  • Focused on durability and reparability to extend product lifespan.
  • Created an affordable and educational system adapted for small urban spaces.

Outcome: stable 24/7 operation, clear dashboards, and healthier plants — while keeping cost, energy and e‑waste in check.
